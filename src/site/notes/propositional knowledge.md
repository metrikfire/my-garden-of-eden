Also known as **Declarative knowledge**, is a type of knowledge that can be expressed in statements which are either true or false. A fundamental aspect of [[epistemology\|epistemology]], and distinct from other types of knowledge such as [[procedural or ability knowledge\|procedural or ability knowledge]] or [[tacit\|tacit]] knowledge.

**Example:**
- The earth is flat
- If you drop a ball, it will fall
