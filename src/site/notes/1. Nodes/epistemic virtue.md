**Description**
A character trait that makes an agent better suited to gaining the truth, thus being prone to better [[1. Nodes/reliabilism\|1. Nodes/reliabilism]]. For example, conscientiousness. An agent who is conscientious in the way in which she forms her belief will more likely to form true beliefs than someone who is unconscientious. Includes cognitive faculties and perceptual faculties (eyesight, etc.)
