**Definition**
the outcome being in direct contradiction from the intent, usually highlighting a third, tangential lesson. For instance, over preparing for a test leading to immobility or freezing when encountering novel questions highlights the third, unspoken concept of *control*.

**Annotated examples**
<blockquote> How sweet it was to die, knowing someone wanted you to live.</blockquote>

~ *Harrow. E. A, Ch. 13, [[4. Artifacts/The Everlasting\|The Everlasting]]*
